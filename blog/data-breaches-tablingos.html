Data Breaches: What You Need to Know

Ibrahim Khawar

Published on August 29, 2024

In recent months, several high-profile companies have fallen victim to devastating data breaches, exposing the sensitive information of millions of customers. As a leading data management platform, Tablingos is committed to helping its customers navigate these turbulent waters and safeguard their data.


The Okta Breach

Okta, the identity and access management company, reported a security breach in October 2023 where hackers gained access to private customer information through its customer support management system. The attackers were able to view content uploaded by Okta customers, including HTTP archive (HAR) files that can contain sensitive data such as cookies and session tokens. Okta has since revoked embedded session tokens and advised customers to sanitize credentials within HAR files before sharing.


The breach at Okta is particularly concerning as the company is a trusted provider of identity and access management services to thousands of organizations worldwide. The fact that hackers were able to infiltrate Okta's systems and access customer data highlights the vulnerability of even the most secure platforms.


One of the key lessons from the Okta breach is the importance of maintaining strict access controls and monitoring for suspicious activity within customer support systems. Okta has acknowledged that the breach occurred due to a lapse in its own security protocols, and the company has since implemented additional measures to prevent similar incidents in the future.


The 23andMe Breach

In December 2023, genetic testing firm 23andMe revealed that a hacker or hackers had accessed nearly seven million customer profiles, including ancestry reports, zip codes, and birth years. The breach occurred through a technique called credential stuffing, where hackers reused old usernames and passwords from other websites to break into 23andMe accounts. 23andMe has since required all customers to reset their passwords and use two-step verification.


The 23andMe breach is a stark reminder of the importance of robust password management and the need for multi-factor authentication. Credential stuffing attacks are becoming increasingly common, and companies must take proactive steps to protect their customers' accounts.


For Tablingos customers, this breach highlights the importance of implementing strong password policies and encouraging the use of two-factor authentication. By ensuring that your customers' login credentials are secure, you can significantly reduce the risk of similar breaches occurring within your own organization.


The Air Europa Breach

Most recently, in March 2024, International Consolidated Airlines Group S.A. warned Air Europa customers that their personal data, including passport information, identity cards, birthdays, names, nationalities, and phone numbers, may have been compromised due to a security incident discovered in October 2023.


The Air Europa breach is particularly concerning as it involves the exposure of sensitive personal information, such as passport details. This type of data can be used for identity theft, fraud, and other malicious activities, putting affected customers at significant risk.


For Tablingos customers, the Air Europa breach underscores the importance of implementing robust data security measures, particularly when handling sensitive personal information. This may include encryption, access controls, and regular security audits to ensure that your customers' data is protected from unauthorized access or misuse.
